MBTI Practical Application

MBTI Daily Uses

  1. Better understand yourself and reduce internal friction: Understand your E/I and T/F preferences, accept your energy pattern, stop forcing yourself to live like others, and reduce self-doubt and internal friction.
  2. Improve communication, reduce conflicts and enhance understanding: Understand others' thinking modes and behavioral preferences, realize that their choices are not directed at you, reduce interpersonal misunderstandings, and make getting along smoother.
  3. Make choices more easily: Career, study and life plans are more in line with your own personality. For example, you can figure out whether you are more suitable for independent research or team collaboration, and whether you prefer a clear plan or a flexible and casual life, avoiding blind following.
  4. Improve learning and work efficiency: Adjust your way of doing things according to your own personality preferences. For example, I-types are suitable for completing tasks quietly and independently, E-types are suitable for inspiring inspiration in team collaboration, J-types are good at advancing according to plans, and P-types are suitable for flexibly responding to unexpected situations, so as to accurately match scenarios and improve efficiency.
  5. Improve intimate relationships and friendships: Respect each other's personality differences, stop demanding others according to your own standards, and establish a comfortable, real and long-lasting way of getting along.

Common Misconceptions

MBTI can determine fate / predict the future

Fact: MBTI is a personality preference test, not an ability test, let alone a fate judgment. Personality does not determine success or failure; environment, efforts and choices are far more important than personality type.

Some types are better than others

Fact: There is no hierarchy or superiority among the 16 personality types. Introversion is not worse than extroversion, and sensibility is not weaker than rationality. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages and its own value.

MBTI results are always unchangeable

Fact:Fact: MBTI test results will change with life experience, living environment and age growth. It reflects "you at the moment", not "you forever". Major life events, changes in social roles, or deliberate self-development can all subtly shift one's personality preferences over time.

Use MBTI to label or discriminate against others

Fact:MBTI is a tool for understanding others, not a standard for judging others. You cannot define a person's character, ability and sense of responsibility with labels such as "all INTPs are lazy" or "all ESTJs are domineering".

MBTI is an either-or choice

Fact:Most people are in an intermediate state, not absolutely E or absolutely I, nor absolutely T or absolutely F. MBTI is a personality tendency, not a shackle that binds people.

MBTI can be used to match love / friendship

Fact: There is no "naturally perfect match" personality combination, nor "naturally incompatible" types. The quality of a relationship depends on mutual respect, communication and values, not on personality letters.
